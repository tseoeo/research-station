# Príncipe Real Food Options + Mercado de Campo de Ourique

## Near Jardim Botânico (Príncipe Real) — local, not expensive

### Faz Frio — Rua Dom Pedro V 96
- **150+ years of history**, opened 1870s. Reborn 2018 after restoration.
- Historic tasca, literary gathering spot, sailors' port, Portuguese guitar music in the old days.
- **Bacalhau specialist**: bacalhau à brás, bacalhau à Zé do Pipo, bacalhau com todos.
- **~€20/person.** No reservations, first-come-first-served.
- **Tue/Wed/Sun 12:00-00:00, Thu/Fri/Sat 12:00-02:00.** Continuous service. Closed Mondays.
- ~2 min walk from Jardim Botânico.

### Tascardoso — Rua do Século 242 (also entrance on Rua Dom Pedro V)
- **Opposite Jardim do Príncipe Real.** Same owner 40+ years, staff 10+ years.
- 30 seats. Two entrances (main one marked by azulejos and wisteria).
- Dishes from all regions of Portugal: corvina, chocos assados com tinta (squid in own ink), bochechas de porco (pork cheeks), grilled octopus, bife à portuguesa, arroz doce.
- **€12-20/dish, ~€22/person full meal with wine + dessert.**
- **Mon-Sat 12:00-15:00 and 19:00-23:30.** NOT continuous — closed 15-19.

### Confeitaria Cistér — Rua da Escola Politécnica 107
- Founded **1838**. Same street as Jardim Botânico, 50m from entrance.
- No-frills café with traditional Portuguese pastries + simple lunch dishes.
- **Mon-Sat 6am-midnight continuous.** Cheapest option: €10-15 for lunch, €3-5 for counter snacks.

### "Interesting" picks (chef-driven, slightly pricier)

**A Cevicheria** (Rua Dom Pedro V 129) — Chef Kiko Martins. Peruvian-Portuguese ceviche fusion. Giant octopus sculpture on the ceiling. Walk-in, 15-40 min wait common. Mon-Sun 12:30-midnight. ~€25-30/person.

**Tapisco** (Rua Dom Pedro V 81) — Chef Henrique Sá Pessoa (Michelin 2-star Alma). Spanish tapas + Portuguese petiscos + Lisbon's first vermouth bar. Walk-in friendly. ~€20-30/person.

**Lost In** (Rua Dom Pedro V 56) — Quirky "lost in India" themed cafe. Best terrace view in Príncipe Real over Avenida da Liberdade and the castle. Medium food quality, high view quality. Happy hour Mon-Fri 16-18, all cocktails 50% off.

## Mercado de Campo de Ourique — food hall guide

**Rua Coelho da Rocha 104, Campo de Ourique.** Traditional market since 1934, food hall added 2013.

### Hours
- Tuesday: 10:00-23:00
- General: Mon-Thu 10-23, Fri-Sat 10-24, Sun 10-17 (varies)

### How it works
- ~25 food stalls in the center, communal tables in the middle
- Traditional fishmongers/butchers/greengrocers on the outer ring (morning-focused)
- Order from multiple stalls, bring plates to shared tables
- Pay per stall (each has own register)
- Most accept cards, keep small cash handy
- Wine by the glass ~€3-5, beer ~€2

### What to eat (general categories — stall lineup rotates)
- **Seafood/marisqueira stall**: fresh fish, grilled octopus, clams, oysters if available
- **Petiscos**: pastéis de bacalhau from the fryer, rissóis, croquetes
- **Pica-pau**: beef tenderloin strips with pickles and spices — classic Portuguese petisco
- **Grilled meats**: entrecôte, possibly leitão (suckling pig)
- **Pastéis de nata / pastry** from the dessert counter
- **Portuguese wine by the glass**: vinho verde with seafood, young Alentejo/Douro red with meat

### What to skip
- Sushi/pizza/burger stalls — not why you came
- International stalls in general — filter for Portuguese-only

### Getting there
- **Tram 28 to western terminus (Campo de Ourique / Prazeres)** — 1 block from market. The tram ride IS the sightseeing.
- Taxi/Uber from Saldanha: ~€8-10, 10-12 min

### Pairs well with
- **Cemitério dos Prazeres** (5 min walk) — 1833 Père-Lachaise-style cemetery, atmospheric
- **Casa Fernando Pessoa** (same street as the market) — poet's last apartment, Rua Coelho da Rocha 16

### Honest caveat
Stall lineups change constantly. The specific stalls and dishes described above are general categories based on the market type. Check @mercadodecampodeourique on Instagram or walk the loop before committing.
