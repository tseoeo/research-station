# Lisbon Breakfast — Authentic Morning Spots

## What Portuguese breakfast actually is

Portuguese breakfast (pequeno-almoço) is simple, fast, cheap, and standing up. Walk into a pastelaria, go to the counter ("ao balcão" — costs less than sitting), point, order a coffee + pastry/toast, eat in 10 minutes, leave. Total: €3-4/person.

## What to order

### Coffee
- **Bica** — Lisbon word for espresso. Short, strong.
- **Meia de leite** — half coffee, half hot milk, ceramic cup. The most common Portuguese breakfast coffee.
- **Galão** — weaker, milkier, in a tall glass. Like a latte.
- **Abatanado** — Americano / long black.

### Food
- **Pastel de nata** — custard tart. Dust with cinnamon + powdered sugar.
- **Bolo de arroz** — rice-flour muffin in paper wrapper. The most actually-Portuguese everyday breakfast pastry tourists never order.
- **Torrada com manteiga** — thick-cut buttered toast. Special double-thick pastelaria bread. Local ritual: save the soft center strip to eat last.
- **Queque** — small pound-cake muffin, good for dipping into meia de leite.
- **Croissant português** — softer, slightly sweeter than French. Benard is famous for theirs.
- **Tosta mista** — grilled ham + cheese sandwich.

## The historic pastelarias (central Lisbon)

### Pastelaria Versailles — Av. da República 15A, Avenidas Novas
- Founded **1922**. Art Nouveau interior: high ceilings, huge mirrors, carved wood, marble, brass.
- Known locally as "the great lady" of Lisbon pastelarias.
- **Opens 7:30am Mon-Sat.** Much less touristy because it's in Avenidas Novas.
- Near **Saldanha metro** (Yellow + Red lines).
- Best pick for the grandeur experience without the tourist tableau.

### A Brasileira — Rua Garrett 120, Chiado
- Founded **1905**. Art Deco interior unchanged: wooden booths, mirrored walls, brass fittings, long oak bar.
- **Fernando Pessoa actually drank here.** The bronze statue on the terrace is there because he was a real regular.
- **Opens 7:00am.** Go before 9am and stand at the counter — the inside bar is still where Chiado locals stop.
- Terrace is touristy and more expensive — skip the terrace, do the counter.

### Pastelaria Benard — Rua Garrett 104, Chiado
- Founded **1868**. Originally a French bakery (Elie Benard, French immigrant). Was high society's tea room until WWII.
- **Famous for croissants** — plain, chocolate, egg cream, strawberry. The croissants are the reason to come.
- **Opens 8:30am Mon-Sat.** Literally next door to A Brasileira. Quieter, more civilized.

### Confeitaria Nacional — Praça da Figueira 18B, Baixa
- Founded **1829** — one of the oldest pastelarias in Europe. Was literally the royal bakery.
- **Opens 8:00am.** Ornate interior, crystal chandeliers, polished wood.
- Famous for bolo-rei (Christmas), palmier recheado, bolo de arroz.
- Right next to Casa das Bifanas on the same square.

## Non-touristy neighborhood picks

### Pastelaria Aloma — Campo de Ourique (Rua Francisco Metrass 67)
- Founded 1943. **Won Lisbon's annual blind pastel de nata competition three times** (2012, 2013, 2015).
- Zero tourists, proper neighborhood pastelaria. Open early morning.

### Confeitaria Cistér — Rua da Escola Politécnica 107, Príncipe Real
- Founded **1838**. No-frills café on the same street as Jardim Botânico.
- **Mon-Sat 6am-midnight continuous.** Cheapest of any option — €3-5 for counter snacks.

## What NOT to do for breakfast
- Skip "brunch" restaurants (Dear Breakfast, Nicolau, Hello Kristof, etc.) — Australian-style brunch for €15+. Authentic Lisbon breakfast is €3 at a counter.
- Skip A Brasileira's outdoor terrace — double the price.
- Skip Time Out Market for breakfast.
