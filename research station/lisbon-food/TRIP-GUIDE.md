## ⚠️ Read this first: Monday is a trap day

**Closed on Mondays** (three of the famous ones):
- **Cervejaria Ramiro** — seafood + beer
- **O Velho Eurico** — neo-tasca, polvo à lagareiro
- **Zé dos Cornos** — grilled pork ribs

If you only have Monday to eat, these three are OUT. If you have Tue–Sat, slot them in then.

## The Monday-proof itinerary

### Lunch (get there by 12:00-12:15)

**→ Zé da Mouraria** — Rua João do Outeiro 24, Mouraria
The taxi drivers' tasca. No reservations, cash only, queue forms fast. Order **bacalhau à minhota** (cod with chickpeas). Portions are for two+. Endorsed by Lisbon's top chefs. Closed Sundays.

*Or, if you're in Campo de Ourique:*

**→ Imperial de Campo de Ourique / Tasca do João** — Rua Correia Teles 67
Family-run since 1985, Minho-style cooking. Menu rotates by day. Order **chanfana** (goat stew), **bolo de bolacha** for dessert. Mon–Sat 10–17. Closed Sundays.

### Afternoon walking crawl (Baixa → Chiado)

Do this in one loop, 60–90 minutes, all stops 5–10 min apart:

1. **Casa das Bifanas** — Praça da Figueira 7A. Start with a bifana. Say "com mostarda e piri-piri." ~€2. Open 6:30–midnight daily.
2. **A Tendinha do Rossio** — pastel de bacalhau, €1.50–3. Crispy, old-school.
3. **Ginjinha Espinheira** — Largo São Domingos 8. Stand outside with the crowd. Order "**uma ginjinha, com elas**" (with a cherry). €1.50-ish.
4. **Nova Pombalina** — Rua do Comércio 2. Order **sandes de leitão** (roast suckling pig sandwich). Cash only. 60 seconds to hand. Open 7:30–19:30 Mon–Sat.
5. **Manteigaria Chiado** — Rua do Loreto 2. Watch them make pastéis de nata through the glass. Dust with cinnamon + sugar at the counter. Open 8am–midnight.
6. **O Trevo** — Praça Luís Camões. Another bifana, if you have room. Bourdain called them "hot, porky love."

### Dinner

**→ Casa dos Passarinhos** — Rua Silva Carvalho 195, Campo de Ourique
Open since 1923. **Choco frito** (fried cuttlefish), **naco na pedra** (steak on stone), **monkfish rice with prawns**. No reservations, always full. Open Mon 12–15 and 19:00–22:30.

**→ OR Marisqueira do Lis** — Av. Almirante Reis 27B
Since 1973. 250m from the (closed-Monday) Ramiro. Order **gambas ao alho** (garlic prawns) and **percebes** (barnacles) with cold Sagres. Open Mon 9:00–1:00. *Closed Tuesdays.*

## Tue–Sat additions

Only if your trip extends beyond Monday:

- **Zé dos Cornos** (Beco dos Surradores 5, Mouraria) — charcoal pork ribs, €13 share plate, beer in the street while you wait. Tue–Sat lunch; Fri–Sat also dinner.
- **O Velho Eurico** (Largo São Cristóvão 3, Mouraria) — neo-tasca. **Order polvo à lagareiro** (octopus). Reservations via Instagram DM weeks ahead, or show up at 12:30 opening. Tue–Sat 12:30–15, 20–22:30.
- **Cervejaria Ramiro** (Av. Almirante Reis 1H) — seafood + beer since 1956. Finish with a **prego** for dessert (it's a tradition). Expect a wait; book via Fork. Tue–Sun 12–00:30.

## Insider local picks (Portuguese-press favorites, barely in English sources)

These need a phone call for hours. Worth it if you want to eat where no English-speaking tourists are.

- **Tasca do Miguel** — Rua Egas Moniz 45A, Areeiro. **Won the national "Tascando" best-tasca vote.** 30 years, 150 meals/day, purely neighborhood clientele. Grills + cozido à portuguesa under €10. Phone: 218404657.
- **O Trigueirinho** — Mouraria back alleys. "Hidden family restaurant," zero English coverage, Portuguese sources rave.
- **Stop do Bairro** — Rua Tenente Ferreira Durão 55A, Campo de Ourique. Soccer memorabilia on the walls, typed daily-specials menu in Portuguese only.
- **Solar dos Mouros** — Penha de França. Big aluminum platters, 11–22:00, almost no tourists.

## Hard skips

- **Sardines** — wrong season. Wait until August.
- **Casa Portuguesa do Pastel de Bacalhau** (Rua Augusta) — tourist trap with queijo-da-serra filling; not traditional.
- **Time Out Market** — overpriced, crowded, everything there is better at its source. Exception: Manteigaria has a counter there with the same tarts.
- **Any restaurant on Rua Augusta, Praça do Comércio, or touristy Alfama drags.**
- **Restaurant Sol Nascente** (Alfama, Rua S. Tome) — specifically flagged for the €9.60-for-bread scam.
- **Any place with**: photos of food outside, laminated menus in 6 languages, waiters calling you in from the street.

## Rules of the road

**Couvert**: Bread, olives, cheese arriving unordered is normal. **You pay if you eat any.** Say "**Não, obrigado**" to remove it. €2/person normal, €5 OK at nicer places, more = walk.

**Cash**: Zé da Mouraria, Nova Pombalina, most old-school tascas are cash-only. Carry €30–50 cash per meal per two people.

**Lunch hours**: Tascas open 12:00, fill by 12:30, have a line by 13:00. **Arrive at 12:00.** Portuguese locals typically eat lunch 13:00–14:30 — you want to be seated before they arrive.

**Dinner hours**: Portuguese dinner is 20:00–22:00, not 18:00. Showing up at 19:00 means empty restaurant and confused waiter. Aim for 20:00–20:30.

**Snails ("caracóis")**: If you see a hand-written "**Há caracóis!**" sign on a tasca door, the season has started. Order a small plate (caneca), suck them from the shell, drink beer. Don't chase them — April is the very beginning, quality peaks later.

**Ordering tips**:
- "Meia dose" = half portion. Portions are huge, meias doses often suffice.
- "Do dia" = dish of the day, written on paper/chalk. Usually the best call.
- "Para partilhar" = to share. Saying this at a tasca tells them you're smart.

## Neighborhoods, quick reference

| Neighborhood | Why go | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Mouraria** | Tasca capital of Lisbon | Zé da Mouraria (lunch), O Velho Eurico + Zé dos Cornos (Tue–Sat) |
| **Campo de Ourique** | "Most village-like" area, few tourists | Imperial/Tasca do João, Casa dos Passarinhos, Stop do Bairro |
| **Baixa / Chiado** | Street food + pastry crawl | Bifana/ginjinha/pastel circuit |
| **Areeiro** | Pure local, no tourists | Tasca do Miguel |
| **Graça** | Quiet alternative to Alfama | O Satélite, O Pitéu |
| **Belém** | Monastery + pasteis pilgrimage | Pastéis de Belém (go INSIDE, not the takeaway line) |

## Key dishes glossary

- **Bifana** — pork-in-a-roll, garlic+wine marinated. Add mustard + piri-piri.
- **Prego** — steak-in-a-roll, garlicky. Often eaten as "dessert" after seafood.
- **Sandes de leitão** — roast suckling pig in a roll. Richer treat, pricier.
- **Pastel de nata** — egg custard tart, flaky shell. Cinnamon + sugar on top.
- **Pastel de bacalhau** — cod-and-potato fritter. Standing snack, €1.50–3.
- **Bacalhau à minhota** — roasted cod with chickpeas + mashed potato (Zé da Mouraria specialty).
- **Bacalhau à brás** — shredded cod with onion, matchstick potato, scrambled eggs.
- **Polvo à lagareiro** — octopus roasted with olive oil, garlic, smashed potatoes.
- **Chanfana** — goat stewed in red wine (Tasca do João specialty).
- **Choco frito** — breaded + fried cuttlefish (Casa dos Passarinhos specialty).
- **Caracóis** — seasonal snails with garlic, piri-piri, oregano.
- **Gambas ao alho** — garlic prawns.
- **Percebes** — gooseneck barnacles. Suck the meat out. Expensive.
- **Ginjinha** — sour cherry liqueur, served in 1.5cl shot glasses.
- **Couvert** — bread/cheese/olives brought unordered, charged separately.
