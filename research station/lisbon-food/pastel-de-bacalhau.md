# Best Pastel de Bacalhau in Lisbon — Deep Research

## What makes a great one
1. **Cod-to-potato ratio** — at least 50/50 by weight, ideally more cod. Cheap versions are 80% potato.
2. **Freshness** — made to order and fried on demand vs pre-made/frozen/reheated. The single biggest quality variable.
3. **No added cheese.** Traditional version is cod, potato, parsley, egg. Cheese-stuffed versions (2015 invention) are explicitly not traditional.
4. **Crispy exterior, moist-not-dry interior, visible flakes of cod** when you bite in.

## History
Carlos Bento da Maia codified the recipe in his 1904 cookbook *Tratado de Cozinha e Copa*. Originated in Northern Portugal (where it's called "bolinhos de bacalhau") with the arrival of the potato. Became a national classic in Lisbon's tascas and taverns.

## Ranked recommendations

### 🥇 A Tendinha do Rossio — the traditional standing-bar classic
- **Rua Betesga 4 / Praça Dom Pedro IV, Rossio** (north side, next to Teatro Nacional)
- **Founded 1818 or 1840** (sources vary) — over 180 years old.
- Cultural significance: painter José Malhoa met fado singer Amâncio here, who posed for the famous painting *O Fado* (now in the Fado Museum).
- Tiny standing-bar tavern. Walk in, order at the counter, stand, eat, leave. €1.50-3 per pastel.
- The honest, traditional, authentic standing-bar pastel made in-house.

### 🥈 Laurentina, O Rei do Bacalhau — the sit-down restaurant
- **Av. Conde Valbom 71A, Avenidas Novas** (~10 min walk from Saldanha hotel)
- Since **1976**. Founded by António Pereira who ran Leão d'Ouro in Lourenço Marques (Mozambique) for 22 years.
- "The King of Cod" — most extensive bacalhau menu in the city: bolinhos de bacalhau, bacalhau com natas, bacalhau à brás, bacalhau à Zé do Pipo, bacalhau com todos.
- **Mon-Sat 12:00-16:00 and 19:00-23:00. Closed Sundays.**
- Reservation recommended for dinner. ~€30-40/person.
- TasteAtlas Recommended.

### 🥉 Casa Portuguesa do Pastel de Bacalhau — the famous brand (with caveats)
- **Rua Augusta 106, Baixa** (plus 9 other locations)
- Won NiT reader poll with **57% of votes, 30% ahead of second place**.
- ~5,500 pastels/day, ~2 million/year across 10 locations.
- Famous for **Queijo da Serra cheese-stuffed version** (introduced 2015). Not traditional.
- **Caveats**: reader poll ≠ blind tasting; industrial scale = reheated; on Rua Augusta tourist strip.
- Decent to good. Try the traditional AND cheese versions side by side for ~€5 total.

### Honorable mentions
- **O Beco** (Alfama, Beco do Espírito Santo 9) — hidden alley spot, watch the chef, mixed reviews on the pastel specifically (some say "impressive," others "average")
- **A Modesta da Pampulha** — NiT-endorsed "tasca obrigatória," no frozen/canned, everything fresh
- **As Salgadeiras** (Bairro Alto) — former bakery, home vibe

## The home truth
The best pastel de bacalhau is usually made by someone's grandmother in a tasca nobody has heard of, fried to order, eaten at 35°C twelve seconds out of the fryer, with a cold beer. Every "best of" list overweights famous places. Walk into any non-tourist pastelaria at 11:30am and ask for a "pastel de bacalhau acabado de sair" and you'll probably get one as good as anything on this list, for €1.50.
