# Angle 2: Taxonomy of Multiplication — Research Landscape

> **Core claim**: If AI is an accelerator/impact multiplier, we need a structured map of WHAT it multiplies, HOW, BY HOW MUCH, and FOR WHOM. Without this, we cannot aim it.

> **Key finding from this research**: No canonical taxonomy of AI multiplication modes exists. The literature fragments across economics (automation vs. augmentation), organizational science (Centaurs vs. Cyborgs), and philosophy (amplification vs. mediation). The gap we identified is real — and the 2025-2026 empirical data makes it urgent, because AI multiplication turns out to be not a scalar but a vector with dimensions pointing in opposite directions simultaneously.

## I. SUPPORTING

Work that directly argues or provides evidence for the claim that we need a structured taxonomy of what AI multiplies.

### A. The General-Purpose Technology Foundation

**Bresnahan & Trajtenberg, "General Purpose Technologies: Engines of Growth" (1995)**
The canonical framework defining what makes a technology a "multiplier." Three criteria: (1) **pervasiveness** — used as inputs across many sectors, (2) **inherent potential for technical improvement**, and (3) **innovational complementarities** — productivity of downstream R&D increases as the GPT improves. This is the closest economics has come to defining the *structure* of technological multiplication, but it describes *conditions for* multiplication, not *modes of* multiplication. It tells you a GPT will multiply broadly; it does not tell you what dimensions get multiplied or by how much.

**Eloundou, Manning, Mishkin, Rock, "GPTs are GPTs" (Science, 2024)**
Empirically tested whether LLMs qualify as a GPT. Found ~80% of the US workforce could have at least 10% of tasks affected; ~46% could see over half their tasks impacted when including complementary software. Confirms the pervasiveness criterion. But the paper's very title — a clever pun — reveals the conceptual gap: "GPT" as an economic category tells you something will be pervasive and complementary but not *how* it will multiply in each domain.

**Agrawal, Gans & Goldfarb, "Prediction Machines" (2018) / "Power and Prediction" (2022)**
Reframes AI as a collapse in the cost of *prediction*. Every decision = prediction + judgment. AI cheapens prediction, which changes optimal decision architecture. This is one of the few frameworks that identifies a *specific mode* of multiplication (prediction cost collapse) and traces its second-order effects (system redesign, new decision architectures). But it covers only one mode — prediction — and does not attempt to taxonomize the full landscape.

### B. The Empirical Case for Differential Multiplication

**Brynjolfsson, Li, Raymond, "Generative AI at Work" (QJE, 2025)**
The landmark study: 5,172 customer-support agents, staggered AI rollout. 14% average productivity increase; **34% for novice workers, near-zero for top performers**. Mechanism: AI disseminates tacit knowledge of top performers to novices. This is not just "faster" — it is a specific multiplication mode: **skill compression**. The study demonstrates empirically that what AI multiplies depends on who you are and what you already have. It multiplies the gap between your current performance and the frontier — the bigger the gap, the bigger the multiplication.

**Dell'Acqua, Mollick et al., "Navigating the Jagged Technological Frontier" (Organization Science, 2025)**
758 BCG consultants with GPT-4. Tasks completed 12.2% more, 25.1% faster, at 40%+ higher quality — *inside the frontier*. Outside the frontier, performance dropped 19%. The "jagged frontier" concept is itself an argument for taxonomy: AI's multiplication boundary is irregular, unpredictable, and does not map onto human intuitions about task difficulty. Without a structured map, you cannot know where you stand relative to the frontier.

**Randazzo, Mollick et al., "Cyborgs, Centaurs and Self-Automators" (HBS Working Paper, 2024-2025)**
Extended the BCG study to identify three modes of human-AI collaboration:
- **Cyborgs** (~60%): Continuous iterative dialogue with AI. Highest overall output. Developed new AI-related skills.
- **Centaurs** (~14%): Selective AI use with firm human control. Highest accuracy. Deepened domain expertise.
- **Self-Automators** (~27%): Delegated entire workflows. Developed *neither* AI skills *nor* domain skills.

This is the closest thing to a taxonomy of *collaboration modes*, but it describes how humans *use* AI, not what AI *multiplies*. The implication is crucial though: the same tool produces divergent multiplication depending on the mode of engagement.

**Doshi & Moore, "Toward a Human-AI Task Tensor" (arXiv 2503.15490, 2025)**
The most explicit attempt at structured taxonomy found in the literature. Eight dimensions: task definition, AI contribution, interaction modality, audit requirement, output definition, decision-making authority, AI structure, and human persona. Forthcoming in *Handbook of AI and Strategy* (Edward Elgar, 2026). This is a taxonomy of *task configurations*, not multiplication *modes* — it tells you how to set up the human-AI relationship, not what gets amplified. But it is the right instinct: you need multi-dimensional structure, not a single number.

### C. The Productivity Paradox as Evidence of Missing Taxonomy

**NBER CEO Study (January 2026)**
~6,000 executives across US, UK, Germany, Australia. Nearly 90% reported AI had **no measurable impact** on employment or productivity over three years. The Solow paradox redux: "You can see AI everywhere but in the productivity statistics." If we had a taxonomy of multiplication modes, we could diagnose *why* — which modes are active, which are blocked, which are canceling each other out.

**Faros AI Research Report (2025)**
10,000+ developers, 1,255 teams. High-AI-adoption teams completed 21% more tasks, merged 98% more PRs. But PR review time increased 91%, bug rate increased 9%, PR size increased 154%. No correlation between AI adoption and company-level improvement. This is multiplication on one dimension (code output) creating bottlenecks on another (review capacity). Without a taxonomy that tracks multiple dimensions simultaneously, you cannot see the transfer effect.

**BCG "From Potential to Profit" (2025)**
Only 5% of companies are "future-built" value generators from AI. Over 80% reported no meaningful EBIT impact. The gap between AI investors and AI value creators is *widening*. A taxonomy would help explain why: most organizations are measuring the wrong multiplication (output volume) and missing the negative multiplication (quality degradation, coordination overhead, skill atrophy).

## II. CHALLENGING

Work that argues against or complicates the claim — either that AI's effects resist taxonomy, or that the "multiplier" framing itself is wrong.

### A. Latour's Challenge: AI Is Not an Amplifier, It Is a Mediator

**Bruno Latour, Actor-Network Theory**
Latour distinguishes **intermediaries** (pass through without transformation) from **mediators** (transform what they carry). A genuine amplifier would be an intermediary — same signal, bigger. Latour would argue AI is a mediator: it transforms, translates, distorts, and reconfigures what passes through it. If this is right, the "multiplier" metaphor is fundamentally misleading. You are not getting "more of the same" — you are getting something *different*. The taxonomy we need is not of multiplication modes but of *transformation modes*.

This is the strongest philosophical challenge. Partial response: even if AI transforms rather than merely amplifies, the transformation can still be mapped along dimensions (what gets enhanced, what gets distorted, what gets lost). But Latour's point demands honesty: "multiplication" implies neutrality that may not exist.

**Eric Deibel, "AI and Sociotechnical Order: Winner, Latour and the Social Contract" (2025)**
Recent paper applying both Winner and Latour to AI governance. Argues that treating AI as a simple amplifier obscures the political and social arrangements that AI *constructs*, not merely magnifies.

### B. Acemoglu's Skepticism: The Gains Are Smaller Than You Think

**Daron Acemoglu, "The Simple Macroeconomics of AI" (NBER WP 32487, 2024; Economic Policy, 2025)**
Predicts AI will boost total factor productivity by **no more than 0.53-0.66% over 10 years**. His task-based framework identifies two forces:
1. **Displacement effect**: AI automates tasks, shifting content against labor
2. **Reinstatement effect**: New tasks emerge where labor has comparative advantage

Key critique: many AI applications are **"so-so automation"** — they automate tasks without creating large cost savings or new capabilities. A taxonomy of multiplication modes might reveal that most current AI use falls into the "so-so" category: small multipliers applied to narrow task slices, with displacement costs offsetting gains elsewhere.

Acemoglu also warns that some AI-created tasks have **negative social value** (algorithmic manipulation, surveillance). A complete taxonomy would need a category for *destructive* multiplication — AI amplifying capacity for harm.

### C. The Counterevidence: AI as Work Intensifier

**Ranganathan & Ye, "AI Doesn't Reduce Work — It Intensifies It" (HBR, February 2026)**
8-month ethnographic study, ~200 employees at a US tech company. Three forms of intensification:
1. **Task expansion**: Product managers coding, researchers doing engineering — role boundaries dissolve
2. **Blurred boundaries**: Work spills into evenings and weekends
3. **Increased multitasking**: More parallel streams, higher cognitive load

83% of workers said AI *increased* their workload. 62% of associates/entry-level reported burnout vs 38% of C-suite. This challenges the "multiplier" frame: AI may multiply the *scope of what each person attempts* without multiplying their capacity to handle it. The multiplication is real but misdirected — it multiplies expectations, not capabilities.

**BCG, "AI Brain Fry" (March 2026)**
1,488 US workers. Workers using 4+ AI tools saw self-reported productivity **plummet**. High AI oversight associated with 14% more mental effort, 12% greater fatigue, 19% greater information overload. 34% with AI brain fry intended to quit (vs 25% baseline). There is a **diminishing and then negative return curve** to AI tool multiplication. 1-3 tools = positive. 4+ tools = negative through cognitive overload.

**BetterUp/Stanford, "AI-Generated Workslop" (HBR, September 2025)**
41% of workers encounter AI-generated "workslop." Each instance costs ~2 hours of rework. $186/month per employee in invisible costs. AI multiplies OUTPUT VOLUME while multiplying REWORK BURDEN — a negative multiplication that partially offsets production gains.

### D. The METR Shock: AI Makes Experts Slower

**METR, "Measuring the Impact of Early-2025 AI on Experienced Open-Source Developer Productivity" (July 2025, updated February 2026)**
Randomized controlled trial: 16 experienced open-source developers, 246 tasks in mature repositories. AI tools made experienced developers **19% slower**, despite developers predicting a 24% speedup before and estimating a 20% speedup *even after completing tasks*. The perception-reality gap — developers *felt* faster while being slower — is a critical finding about multiplication illusions. For experts in familiar domains, AI may be a **0.8x multiplier** while creating a subjective experience of 1.2x. This is multiplication of *confidence* without multiplication of *capability*.

### E. The Cognitive Atrophy Problem

**Multiple studies (2025-2026)**
- Students perform better WITH AI but worse when AI is removed — AI bypasses cognitive processes essential for skill development
- Prolonged AI exposure leads to measurable memory decline (73-student RCT)
- 27% of BCG consultants who became "Self-Automators" developed neither AI skills nor domain skills
- Zimbabwe university study: generative AI dependency causes critical thinking atrophy

**The temporal multiplication paradox**: Short-term multiplication (performance with AI) = 1.3-1.5x. But if underlying skill base erodes, long-term multiplication (capability without AI) drops below 1.0x. AI may multiply **performance** while dividing **capability**. A taxonomy that only captures the current multiplication factor misses the dynamic trajectory — you need a time dimension.

## III. ADJACENT

Work that addresses related but distinct questions, useful as context for building a taxonomy.

### A. Historical GPT Analogies: How Were Previous Multipliers Taxonomized?

**Elizabeth Eisenstein, "The Printing Press as an Agent of Change" (1980)**
Identified three multiplication modes for print: **dissemination** (speed and scale of copying), **standardization** (fidelity and uniformity), and **preservation** (cumulative accumulation over time). Print's greatest gift to science was not speed but accuracy — printed formulas could be trusted. This is a proto-taxonomy of multiplication modes, and it maps to AI: AI offers dissemination (access to knowledge), something like standardization (consistent quality floor), and a complex relationship with preservation (both extends and threatens institutional memory).

**Jeremiah Dittmar, "Information Technology and Economic Change" (CEPR)**
Three economic multiplication mechanisms for the printing press: (1) human capital development, (2) technological and practice diffusion, (3) localized knowledge exchange (printer's workshops as hubs). Cities adopting printing 1450-1500 saw 60 percentage point growth advantages. The lesson: multiplication operates through infrastructure (skill-building), content (knowledge diffusion), and network effects (hub formation).

**Paul David, "The Dynamo and the Computer" (1990)**
The productivity paradox: electrification took ~40 years to show measurable gains. Three adoption phases: (1) **infrastructure substitution** (dropping a dynamo where a steam engine was), (2) **organizational redesign** (individual motors per machine, enabling new factory layouts), (3) **full systemic transformation** (new business models, workflows, human capital). The key insight: GPTs require **co-invention of complementary processes** before their multiplication potential is realized. We are arguably still in phase 1 with AI — substituting AI where a human was, without redesigning the workflow around AI's actual capabilities.

**Douglas Engelbart, "Augmenting Human Intellect: A Conceptual Framework" (1962)**
The origin of "intelligence amplification" as distinct from artificial intelligence. Engelbart's H-LAM/T system identifies four augmentation means: **artifacts** (physical tools), **language** (symbol systems), **methodology** (procedures and strategies), and **training** (skill development). This four-part taxonomy is directly transferable to AI: AI as artifact, AI as new language/interface, AI as methodology enabler, AI as training accelerator. Engelbart understood that the "system" being augmented includes the human, the tools, *and* the methods — not just the person or the machine.

### B. McLuhan's Tetrad: A Ready-Made Taxonomy

**Marshall McLuhan, Laws of Media / Tetrad (posthumous, 1988)**
Four simultaneous effects of any technology:
1. What does it **enhance/amplify**?
2. What does it **obsolesce** (push aside)?
3. What does it **retrieve** (bring back from earlier obsolescence)?
4. What does it **reverse into** when pushed to extremes?

Applied to LLMs (multiple 2025 analyses):
- **Enhances**: Linguistic productivity, access to knowledge, simulation of conversation
- **Obsolesces**: Memorization, traditional search, institutional expertise gatekeeping, solitary thinking
- **Retrieves**: Pre-literate oral traditions — the sage, the oracle, the dialogue partner
- **Reverses into**: Epistemic confusion, dependency, semantic collapse where language becomes so manipulable that meaning itself decays

The tetrad is useful because it insists that multiplication always has a *shadow*: every enhancement simultaneously obsolesces something and carries the seed of its own reversal. A taxonomy of multiplication that only tracks the positive dimension misses half the picture.

**Howard Gardner (Harvard, 2024)** applies McLuhan to AI: if humans cede cognitive functions entirely, they risk losing capacities through atrophy. Three possible responses: total acceptance, total rejection, selective integration.

### C. Illich's Threshold: When Multiplication Flips to Domination

**Ivan Illich, "Tools for Conviviality" (1973)**
A convivial tool promotes autonomy, empowerment, and enjoyment. Illich argued there is a **"second watershed"** for every tool: initially it extends human capability, but past a certain point it creates dependency and replaces human agency. The tool goes from liberating to oppressive.

**Andreas Beinsteiner, "Conviviality, the Internet, and AI" (Open Cultural Studies, 2021)**
Argues AI presents a crisis for conviviality because self-learning algorithms are not comprehensible to users. If construction and repair of tools requires inaccessible expert knowledge, this "necessarily entails social imbalances in power." AI risks "potentially non-recoverable proletarianization."

Relevance: Illich's framework adds a **threshold dimension** to multiplication. The same tool can be a positive multiplier below the threshold and a negative one above it. The BCG "AI Brain Fry" findings empirically confirm this: 1-3 AI tools = positive multiplication, 4+ = cognitive collapse. Any taxonomy needs a concept of *multiplication limits* — the point where more multiplication becomes worse.

### D. The Inequality Landscape: Who Gets Multiplied More?

**David Autor, "Applying AI to Rebuild Middle Class Jobs" (NBER WP 32140, 2024)**
AI could *reverse* the skill premium by extending human expertise downward — enabling more workers to do higher-stakes work currently limited to elite experts. This is explicitly about **expertise democratization** as a multiplication mode. Contrasts with earlier technology waves that polarized labor markets.

**IMF, "AI Adoption and Inequality" (WP/25/68, April 2025)**
AI could reduce *wage* inequality by displacing high-income tasks. But two countervailing forces: (1) high-income tasks are highly complementary with AI, boosting those workers; (2) high earners benefit more from capital returns. **Capital income and wealth inequality always increase with AI adoption.** The micro-level leveling effect coexists with the macro-level concentration effect.

**Bloom, Prettner, Saadaoui, Veruete — "AI Will Likely Shrink Earnings Inequality" (CEPR, October 2024)**
Unlike industrial robots (which replaced routine low-skill tasks), AI substitutes for nonroutine tasks traditionally performed by high-skill workers. The skill premium faces downward pressure. But wealth inequality increases because AI adoption is capital-intensive.

**Sam Manning, "AI's Impact on Income Inequality in the US" (Brookings, July 2024)**
Exposure to AI productivity gains is concentrated among workers earning $90,000+. Manual/service workers have limited workflow access to AI tools. Distinguishes between "who benefits within a task" (lower-skilled) vs. "who has access to AI-augmented tasks at all" (higher-skilled knowledge workers).

The inequality literature reveals a **paradox of differential multiplication**: AI compresses skill differentials *within* tasks it can reach, while expanding access differentials *across* the economy. Both are multiplication — one equalizing, one concentrating — and they operate simultaneously.

### E. Carlota Perez: Timing Determines Who Gets Multiplied

**Carlota Perez, "Technological Revolutions and Financial Capital" (2002); 2025 interview in Development and Change**
Technology revolutions unfold in two halves:
- **Installation Period** (irruption + frenzy): Speculative capital, infrastructure build-out, benefits concentrated among first movers
- **Deployment Period** (synergy + maturity): Benefits spread broadly — *but only if institutions reform at the turning point*

Current AI position: deep in the **frenzy phase** of installation. Classic symptoms: speculative euphoria, narrow dominant firms (Nvidia, Microsoft, OpenAI), massive infrastructure investment, widening gulf between tech leaders and rest of economy.

The critical prediction: current AI inequality is **structurally expected** during installation. The question is not whether AI is currently unequal (it is, by design of the phase) but what happens at the turning point. Historical pattern: 1847 railway crash led to Victorian golden age; 1929 crash led to New Deal. Without reform at the turning point, the result is "oligarchic stagnation."

Relevance: Perez adds a **temporal-structural dimension** to multiplication taxonomy. Who gets multiplied is partly a function of *where we are in the revolution cycle*, not just intrinsic properties of the technology.

## IV. EXPANDING

Work that takes the idea further than our original framing, or opens new dimensions we had not considered.

### A. Non-Western Frameworks: Redefining What "Multiplication" Means

**Yuk Hui, "The Question Concerning Technology in China" (2016); "Machine and Sovereignty" (2024)**
Core concept: **cosmotechnics** — "the unification of the cosmic and moral order through technical activities." There is no universal "Technology." Technical activities express local moral-cosmological orders. In the Chinese Dao-Qi (道-器) framework, tools are not neutral amplifiers but expressions of cosmic order. Amplification without harmony is a category error.

For our taxonomy: Hui's framework challenges the Western assumption that multiplication is value-neutral (more = better). In the Dao-Qi tradition, the question is not "how much does this multiply?" but "does this multiplication harmonize with the moral order?" A complete taxonomy would need an **alignment dimension** — not just magnitude and direction of multiplication, but its relationship to values.

**Ubuntu Philosophy (African): "I am because we are"**
Shifts the fundamental question from "what does this tool amplify for *me*?" to "how does this tool serve *our collective becoming*?" Three Ubuntu dimensions for AI: communalism/solidarity, consensual decision-making, inclusive benefit distribution. The subject being amplified is the community, not the individual. Any taxonomy of multiplication modes must account for this — Western frameworks implicitly assume an individual human as the unit being multiplied.

**Indian Philosophy: Shakti, Yantra, Tantra**
Shakti represents cosmic creative energy with five manifestations: consciousness, ecstasy, will, knowledge, and action. Yantra (from *yam* = hold + *tra* = instrument) are amplification devices by definition — geometric instruments that "contain, channel, and concentrate energy." The Mantra-Yantra-Tantra system offers a triadic taxonomy: amplification through sound/vibration, form/structure, and methodology/system. Relevance: a pre-modern non-Western tradition already has a taxonomy of amplification modes.

**Japanese Dogu (道具): "Way-Tools"**
The characters combine *do/michi* (way/path, = Chinese Dao) with *gu* (tool/implement). Tools are inherently instruments *of a path*, not neutral objects. Nishida Kitaro's "acting intuition" frames tool-use as reciprocal co-creation — not one-directional amplification but mutual transformation. Relevance: suggests that tools are not amplifiers of arbitrary human intentions but instruments that embody and constrain amplification within a teleological framework. A hammer multiplies your capacity to nail — it does not multiply your capacity in general.

**Islamic Hikmah (Wisdom) Tradition**
Al-Jazari's *Book of Knowledge of Ingenious Mechanical Devices* (1206) embodies a framework where engineering serves both practical and transcendent purposes simultaneously. Amplification of human capability is inseparable from the pursuit of divine wisdom. Relevance: a tradition where "for what purpose does this multiply?" is built into the concept of technology itself, not bolted on as an ethical afterthought.

### B. The Five-Axis Model Emerging from 2025-2026 Data

The latest empirical evidence reveals that AI multiplication is **not a scalar but a vector** with multiple dimensions that can point in opposite directions simultaneously. Any single "productivity number" is misleading by construction. The data supports at least five independent axes:

**Axis 1: Volume vs. Quality**
AI consistently multiplies output volume (1.5-3x) while often dividing output quality (0.6-0.9x). Software: 2x more PRs but 9% more bugs, 91% longer reviews, 154% larger PRs (Faros AI). Science: researchers publish 3x more papers with 5x more citations, but cover less topical ground, cluster around data-rich problems, and spark less follow-on engagement (IEEE Spectrum, 2025). Creative work: volume multiplication paired with homogenization.

**Axis 2: Novice vs. Expert**
AI multiplies novice performance (1.2-1.35x) while providing near-zero or negative multiplication for experts (0.8-1.0x). Customer service: 34% for novices, ~0% for experts (Brynjolfsson). BCG consultants: 43% for bottom performers, 17% for top (Mollick). Coding: 27-39% for juniors, 8-13% for seniors; experienced open-source devs: -19% (METR). AI compresses skill distributions rather than raising ceilings.

**Axis 3: Short-term Performance vs. Long-term Capability**
AI multiplies immediate performance (1.2-1.5x) while potentially eroding underlying capabilities over time (<1.0x). Students perform better with AI but worse when it is removed. 27% of BCG Self-Automators developed neither AI nor domain skills. The multiplication factor has a **time dimension** — positive now, potentially negative later.

**Axis 4: Individual vs. Organizational**
AI multiplies individual output (1.1-1.4x) while organizational productivity stays flat (~1.0x). 90% of firms report no measurable productivity impact (NBER, 2026). 80%+ report no EBIT impact (BCG). The gains at the individual level are absorbed by bottleneck transfer, coordination overhead, workslop rework, and review burden.

**Axis 5: Inside vs. Outside the Frontier**
AI multiplies performance on tasks inside its capability frontier (1.3-1.5x) while degrading performance outside it (~0.8x). The frontier is jagged and unpredictable — the same person doing seemingly similar tasks experiences both multiplication and division.

### C. Kentaro Toyama's Law of Amplification

**Toyama, "Geek Heresy" (2015)**
"Technology is only an amplifier of human conditions." Technology magnifies existing capacities and motivations — both positive and negative. It cannot substitute for human wisdom. Despite four decades of innovation, US poverty rates remained stagnant at ~13%. Technology amplified existing social forces without transforming underlying conditions.

This is the strongest form of the "multiplier" argument: technology is *only* an amplifier. It cannot create what is not there. Applied to AI: if you have good judgment, AI multiplies it. If you have poor judgment, AI multiplies that too. If you have access to resources, AI multiplies your advantage. If you lack access, AI multiplies your exclusion. The taxonomy question becomes: what *human conditions* is AI multiplying, and what does the distribution of those conditions look like?

### D. The Amplification-Equalization Paradox

**Bigoni et al. (2025), via Emergent Mind synthesis**
Identified two opposing modes:
- **Amplification mode**: AI complements skill, increases outcome variance (the skilled get more skilled, the unskilled stay behind)
- **Equalization mode**: AI substitutes for skill, decreases outcome variance (everyone converges toward a mean)

Three emergent paradoxes:
1. **Bias magnification**: Output disparity exceeds training data disparity — AI doesn't just reproduce biases, it amplifies them
2. **The expertise paradox**: Experts codify tacit knowledge that enables automation of their own roles
3. **The productivity-competence gap**: Performance rises while underlying expertise falls

### E. Emerging Taxonomy Attempts (2025-2026)

**Tao An, "AI as Cognitive Amplifier" (arXiv, December 2025)**
Three layers of human contribution: (1) problem definition and intent, (2) quality evaluation and judgment, (3) iterative refinement and direction. Three levels of AI engagement: (1) passive acceptance, (2) iterative collaboration, (3) cognitive direction. Grounded in Engelbart's 1962 framework. Key insight: "AI tools do not replace human intelligence — they amplify it. And amplifiers, by their nature, magnify the quality of their input."

**Cognitive Amplification vs. Cognitive Delegation Metric Framework (arXiv, March 2026)**
Four metrics: Cognitive Amplification Index (CAI*), Dependency Ratio (D), Human Reliance Index (HRI), and Human Cognitive Drift Rate (HCDR). Distinguishes human-dominant, balanced collaboration, and AI-dominated regimes. Critical insight: systems can achieve high performance while drifting toward cognitive delegation if HCDR becomes negative — "unsustainable human-AI partnerships."

**Qinshu Yu, "From Efficiency to Symbiosis" (SSRN, 2025-2026)**
Reframes AI as a "meaning amplifier" — not merely accelerating cognitive tasks but reshaping how humans construct meaning. Goes beyond efficiency to address existential and hermeneutic dimensions of multiplication.

**Ledingham et al., "Beyond Automation" (arXiv 2512.05659, 2025)**
UK Government Digital Service study of 193,497 job vacancies. Three modes of AI impact: (1) task automation, (2) task optimization, (3) task reallocation. Redesigned jobs converge on tasks where humans have comparative advantage: strategic leadership, complex problem resolution, stakeholder management.

**DORA Report 2025 (Google)**
Key quote: AI "doesn't create organizational excellence — it amplifies what already exists." Introduced seven team archetypes. Well-functioning teams get better with AI; poorly-functioning teams get worse faster. This directly supports Toyama's Law: AI is an amplifier of existing organizational conditions.

### F. Satya Nadella / Microsoft Pivot (2026)
Publicly reframed AI as a "cognitive amplifier" rather than autonomous intelligence. Signals industry adoption of the amplification frame. Focus on "measurable, real-world value rather than chasing headline model scores." Notable because it represents the corporate mainstream catching up with the academic insight — but without the taxonomy needed to operationalize the frame.

## V. THE GLOBAL DIMENSION: Who Gets Multiplied on the World Stage?

### The North-South Multiplication Gap

**IMF, "The Global Impact of AI: Mind the Gap" (WP/25/76, April 2025)**
Multi-sector dynamic general equilibrium model: AI will **exacerbate cross-country income inequality**, with growth impact in advanced economies potentially more than double that in low-income countries. Improvements in AI preparedness can mitigate but not fully offset these disparities.

**Center for Global Development — Schellekens & Skilling (October 2024)**
Three reasons AI may widen global inequality: (1) richer nations capture AI benefits (US: $67.2B investment vs. minimal in LICs); (2) poorer nations lack disruption management capacity; (3) AI threatens traditional development models — Bangladesh garment sector could lose 60% of jobs by 2030.

**World Bank, "Digital Progress and Trends Report 2025"**
China holds 66% of GenAI patent filings (2014-23). India shows highest growth (56% annually). Low-income countries are largely absent from AI innovation.

**OECD, "Emerging Divides in the Transition to AI" (June 2025)**
Cross-country AI adoption gaps *widened* from 2%-16% in 2021 to 4%-28% in 2024. Largest divide is by age (53.6 percentage points), followed by education and income (~21 points each).

**Digital Cooperation Organization, "The AI Divide" (2026)**
Language is an independent barrier. A well-connected speaker of a low-resource language may have less effective AI access than a less technically sophisticated English speaker. This is a multiplication axis the economic literature largely ignores.

### Counter-Trend: Emerging Economy Individual Adoption

**OECD-Cisco (December 2025)**
Surprising finding: emerging economies (India, Brazil, Mexico, South Africa) are *leading* generative AI adoption among individuals — departing from historical technology patterns. But firm-level adoption remains concentrated in advanced economies. Individual adoption without institutional capacity may not translate to economic multiplication.

### Alternative Models

**Nandan Nilekani (India) — Digital Public Infrastructure Framework**
India's approach: start with digital public infrastructure, then layer AI. "AI will concentrate wealth and power unless deliberately channeled through public infrastructure." Advocates "scaling right" (frugal, citizen-focused) over "scaling big." India's AI future will be phone-based, not desktop-based.

**Masakhane Initiative (Africa) — Ubuntu-Informed AI**
Community-driven NLP developing datasets across 40 African languages. Embeds Ubuntu values into development methodology — community-centered, collective benefit over individual extraction.

**African Union, Africa Declaration on AI (Kigali Summit, April 2025)**
Principles of sovereignty, inclusivity, ethical development. Represents an institutional attempt to define multiplication on African terms.

## VII. WHAT'S MISSING: Gaps, Blind Spots, Unanswered Questions

### Gap 1: No Canonical Taxonomy Exists
The central gap we hypothesized is confirmed. The literature fragments into:
- **Economics**: automation vs. augmentation vs. new task creation (Acemoglu/Restrepo)
- **Organizational science**: Centaurs vs. Cyborgs vs. Self-Automators (Mollick et al.)
- **Task configuration**: 8-dimensional tensor (Doshi & Moore)
- **Collaboration metrics**: CAI*, Dependency Ratio, HRI, HCDR (arXiv 2026)
- **Historical analogy**: dissemination/standardization/preservation (Eisenstein on print)
- **Media theory**: enhance/obsolesce/retrieve/reverse (McLuhan)

No one has unified these into a single structured framework of multiplication modes. This is the contribution space.

### Gap 2: The Multi-Dimensional Vector Problem
Almost all measurement treats "AI productivity" as a single number. The 2025-2026 evidence shows it is at minimum a 5-dimensional vector (volume/quality, novice/expert, short-term/long-term, individual/organizational, inside/outside frontier). A taxonomy must represent this multi-dimensionality or it will be useless.

### Gap 3: The Shadow Side of Multiplication Is Under-Theorized
McLuhan's tetrad insists every enhancement has an obsolescence and a reversal. But most empirical work measures only the enhancement. What does AI *obsolesce*? What capacities atrophy? What does it *reverse into* at extremes? The cognitive atrophy and workslop findings are early glimpses, but there is no systematic framework for the shadow dimensions.

### Gap 4: The "For Whom" Question Lacks a Unified Framework
The inequality literature identifies at least six axes of differential multiplication:
1. Within-task skill level (novices gain more)
2. Access to AI-augmentable tasks (knowledge workers have more surface area)
3. Capital vs. labor (capital always gains more)
4. Global North vs. South (advanced economies see 2x+ growth impact)
5. Language (English speakers get multiplied more)
6. Temporal position in revolution cycle (early adopters during installation phase)

These are studied separately. No one has produced an integrated framework showing how they interact.

### Gap 5: Non-Western Frameworks Are Absent from the Mainstream
Yuk Hui's cosmotechnics, Ubuntu's communal framing, the Indian yantra tradition, the Japanese dogu concept — all offer alternative framings of amplification that challenge Western assumptions (individual as unit, more = better, value-neutrality of tools). These perspectives are almost entirely absent from the economics and organizational science literature on AI productivity. A taxonomy that only speaks the language of Western productivity measurement will be incomplete.

### Gap 6: The Transformation vs. Amplification Question Is Unresolved
Latour's distinction between intermediaries and mediators raises a fundamental question: does AI multiply what you already have (amplification), or does it change what you produce into something different (mediation/transformation)? Most empirical work assumes amplification. If Latour is right, we need a taxonomy of *transformation modes*, not just *multiplication modes*. This is not merely academic — it determines whether you can predict AI's effects by looking at inputs, or whether the outputs are irreducibly emergent.

### Gap 7: The Time Dimension Is Critical and Mostly Ignored
Multiplication factors change over time — in at least three ways:
- **Perez's cycle**: who benefits depends on installation vs. deployment phase
- **David's J-curve**: measured productivity may decline before it rises (co-invention lag)
- **Cognitive atrophy**: short-term multiplication can produce long-term capability erosion
A static taxonomy will mislead. The framework needs dynamic dimensions.

### Gap 8: Organizational Multiplication Is Near-Zero and Nobody Knows Why
The micro-macro paradox is the biggest puzzle in the 2025-2026 data: individual productivity gains of 10-35% coexist with organizational productivity gains of ~0%. Possible explanations include bottleneck transfer, coordination overhead, workslop, and the absorptive capacity problem. But no one has produced a systematic account of why individual multiplication fails to compound into organizational multiplication. This is where a taxonomy would have the most practical value.

### Gap 9: Nobody Is Asking About Multiplication of *Harm*
Acemoglu mentions "negative social value" tasks. The bias amplification literature documents how AI magnifies prejudice. But there is no structured framework for *negative* multiplication — how AI multiplies capacity for surveillance, manipulation, discrimination, environmental damage. A complete taxonomy cannot be only about productivity; it must include multiplication of destructive capabilities.

### Gap 10: The Multiplication of *Attention and Desire* Is Missing
AI systems don't just multiply productive capability. They multiply the ability to capture attention, generate desire, create persuasive content, and shape behavior at scale. This is arguably the largest actual multiplication effect of current AI (powering recommendation engines, ad targeting, content generation for marketing), but it barely appears in the "AI productivity" literature because it is not framed as "productivity." A taxonomy limited to work-task productivity misses the biggest multiplication happening in real time.

## VIII. TOWARD A TAXONOMY: Dimensions That a Framework Would Need

Based on this research, a comprehensive taxonomy of AI multiplication modes would need to capture at minimum:

### What Gets Multiplied (The Object)
- Speed/throughput
- Output volume
- Output quality
- Scope of capability (what becomes possible)
- Access (who can do what)
- Skill compression (closing gaps)
- Prediction/decision capacity
- Creative variation
- Persuasive/attention-capturing capacity
- Capacity for harm (surveillance, manipulation, discrimination)

### How It Multiplies (The Mechanism)
- Cost collapse (prediction, production, distribution)
- Tacit knowledge dissemination (Brynjolfsson's mechanism)
- Bottleneck transfer (multiplication in one area creates constraint in another)
- Cognitive offloading (freeing attention for higher-order tasks — or atrophying the muscle)
- Scope expansion (enabling tasks that were previously impossible)
- Intermediation (connecting actors who could not previously connect)

### The Shadow (What Gets Divided)
- Quality when volume increases
- Capability when performance increases (cognitive atrophy)
- Diversity when efficiency increases (homogenization)
- Autonomy when convenience increases (dependency)
- Human judgment when prediction gets cheap (deskilling)
- Trust when AI-generated content proliferates (workslop, epistemic pollution)

### For Whom (The Distribution)
- Novice vs. expert (novices gain more per task)
- Knowledge worker vs. manual worker (knowledge workers have more AI surface area)
- Capital vs. labor (capital always gains)
- Global North vs. South (infrastructure, language, institutional capacity)
- Installation phase vs. deployment phase (Perez)
- Inside vs. outside the jagged frontier

### Over What Timeframe (The Dynamics)
- Immediate (task-level multiplication, measurable now)
- Medium-term (organizational transformation, requires co-invention)
- Long-term (capability trajectory — building or eroding?)
- Cyclical (installation vs. deployment in Perez's framework)

### Against What Values (The Normative Dimension)
- Western productivity (more output per unit input)
- Dao-Qi harmony (does the multiplication align with cosmic/moral order?)
- Ubuntu communalism (does it serve collective becoming?)
- Illich's conviviality (does it promote autonomy or create dependency?)
- Latour's mediation (is it amplifying or transforming — and do we consent to the transformation?)
