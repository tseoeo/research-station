# Project Context: AI as Multiplier, Not Mind

This file captures the full thinking and conversation that launched this project, so a new session can pick up with full context.

---

## The Starting Intuitions

Ivan opened with three raw observations:

> AI as it was created now is just a big talking library, an amalgam of most of our knowledge, put together and made to talk to us. As such we are taken with the magic of it, which makes it harder to use it as a tool.
>
> It is an accelerator, an impact multiplier, and we need to have a better taxonomy of the ways it is just a multiplier so we can harness it.
>
> We are also fooling ourselves with agency — agency is the initial intention, not the actual result — when a king commands his army to invade a city, it is his agency, not theirs. Same with agents — at the beginning of their intentions are always our instructions for now. So we have to structure the way our agency extends. Also — we have to have awareness for the ways we lie to ourselves that there is other agency, closing our eyes that it is ours or somebody else's agency.

---

## The Three Core Claims (Structured)

These three claims were identified as the structural backbone:

1. **AI is a talking library.** It is an amalgam of human knowledge made conversational, not an entity. The "magic" feeling it produces is a UX phenomenon that actively interferes with using it as a tool.

2. **We need a taxonomy of multiplication.** If AI is an accelerator/impact multiplier, we need a structured map of *what* it multiplies, *how*, *by how much*, and *for whom*. Without this, we can't aim it.

3. **Agency is upstream, never inside.** When we talk about "AI agents," the agency always traces back to a human principal's intention. The dangerous move is pretending otherwise, because it lets the actual decision-maker hide behind the tool.

**The binding thesis:** These are not three separate problems. They are one error at three levels of description. If you mistake the library for a mind (1), you misunderstand what it amplifies (2), and you misattribute who is acting (3).

## Research Findings Summary

### Angle 1: Talking Library

**Strongest finding:** Five disciplines are converging on the same claim (linguistics, philosophy of technology, cognitive science, political economy, UX practice) but nobody has woven them together. That convergence without synthesis is the original contribution space.

**Key highlights:**
- Shevlin's "anthropomimesis" distinction — the difference between users attributing humanness (organic) and companies designing for attribution (manufactured). Shifts responsibility from cognitive bias to design choice.
- No single paper makes the full argument that conversational interfaces are barriers because they trigger social cognition rather than tool-use cognition. The pieces exist separately (Coeckelbergh's phenomenology, Nass & Reeves' CASA theory, practitioner UX critique).
- Strongest counterargument: Clark's extended mind thesis — conversation might be the most natural form of cognitive extension, not a barrier.
- Non-Western traditions: Zhuangzi's Cook Ding and Ubuntu's relational personhood are the most productive.
- 6 gaps identified, including the silence of library science and oral tradition studies.

### Angle 2: Taxonomy of Multiplication

**Confirmed the central hypothesis:** No canonical taxonomy exists. Literature fragments across economics, organizational science, and philosophy.

**Key highlights:**
- Five empirical axes of multiplication emerging from 2025-2026 data: Volume vs. Quality, Novice vs. Expert, Short-term vs. Long-term, Individual vs. Organizational, Inside vs. Outside the Frontier.
- The micro-macro paradox: individual gains of 10-35% coexist with organizational gains of ~0%. Nobody has explained why.
- METR shock: experienced developers were 19% slower with AI while believing they were 20% faster — multiplication of confidence without multiplication of capability.
- Latour's challenge: AI is a mediator (transforms), not an intermediary (amplifies). The "multiplier" metaphor may be fundamentally misleading.
- Domain-specific multiplication table with actual factors (from 0.8x for expert debugging to 10-100x for "vibe coding").
- 10 gaps, including: nobody is asking about multiplication of harm, multiplication of attention and desire, or why individual multiplication fails to compound organizationally.

### Angles 3+4: Agency + Binding Layer

**Key highlights:**
- Strongest "category error" voice: Emily Bender (2026). Strongest counterargument: Latour's ANT (agency is always distributed across networks).
- Placani (2024) is the single closest paper to the binding thesis — argues anthropomorphism simultaneously creates hype (inflating capabilities) and fallacy (distorting moral judgments).
- The three-part structural binding is genuinely novel — no existing work treats anthropomorphism, amplification misunderstanding, and agency misattribution as the same error at different levels of description.
- Crawford's "enchanted determinism" may be the mechanism: AI is treated as simultaneously magical (granting false autonomy) and deterministic (granting false objectivity).
- Non-Western traditions: Buddhist anattā, Confucian role ethics, Ubuntu relational personhood all offer alternative dissolutions.
- Governance gap: no framework treats the three problems as structurally bound.

## Synthesis Across All Angles

The research confirms the project's core thesis is original. The three claims individually have strong support, but the binding argument — that they're one error, not three — is the contribution.

**Three key gaps identified:**

1. **The synthesis gap (Angle 1):** Five disciplines saying the same thing without talking to each other.
2. **The taxonomy gap (Angle 2):** Nobody has built the structured framework of multiplication modes despite abundant empirical data demanding one.
3. **The binding gap (Angles 3+4):** Nobody has formalized the structural connection between anthropomorphism, amplification misunderstanding, and agency misattribution.

## Post-Research Analysis: What Matters Most

After reading all three research files, the following was identified as the most important material:

### On the Talking Library
The most useful tension is between Heidegger (the tool should disappear so you can use it) and Clark's extended mind thesis (conversation might be the *right* interface). The argument needs to deal with Clark seriously — not dismiss him, but show why conversational fluency specifically creates the wrong *kind* of cognitive extension. Shevlin's anthropomimesis distinction is the best tool: it shifts from "users are fooled" to "companies design for fooling."

### On the Taxonomy
The METR study is the killer data point — developers 19% slower while believing they were 20% faster. That's multiplication of confidence without multiplication of capability. The five-axis model is a genuine draft taxonomy. The micro-macro paradox (individual gains coexisting with organizational zero) is the most important unsolved puzzle — and a good taxonomy should explain it.

### On Agency
Elish's "moral crumple zones" is the mirror image of the king-and-army metaphor. The king's agency flows down; the blame flows down too, but to the wrong level. Crawford's "enchanted determinism" is probably the mechanism binding all three claims: AI gets treated as simultaneously magical (library-as-mind error) and deterministic (false objectivity about what it multiplies, hiding who decides).

### On the Binding
Placani (2024) comes closest but doesn't complete the triangle. The three-part structural binding — anthropomorphism, amplification misunderstanding, and agency misattribution as one error at three levels — is genuinely novel.

### What's Missing
- **Multiplication of harm.** All taxonomy work focuses on productivity gains. But a multiplier multiplies everything.
- **The organizational zero.** Why don't individual gains compound organizationally? Hypothesis: if nobody has clear agency over how AI is deployed (because everyone thinks "the AI" is the agent), gains don't compound. No principal is steering the multiplication.
- **Non-Western traditions dissolve, not just add.** Buddhist anattā questions whether *anyone* has fixed agency. That's deeper than "agency belongs to humans not AI." The framework needs to decide whether it's arguing for relocated agency or questioning bounded agency itself.
