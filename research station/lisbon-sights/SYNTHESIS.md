# Lisbon Sights — Niche, Weird, Authentic, Behind-the-Scenes

## Scope
Interesting and offbeat things to see in Lisbon. Focus on: dictatorship/colonialism/communism museums, weird niche museums, behind-the-scenes tours, street art, Carnation Revolution sites, African Lisbon, historic cemeteries, hidden gardens, literary spots, quirky engineering.

---

## Political Memory (Dictatorship + Colonialism + Revolution)

### Museu do Aljube — Resistência e Liberdade ⭐
- **Rua de Augusto Rosa 42, Alfama** (next to the Sé cathedral)
- Former PIDE political prison (1928-1965), ~30,000 political prisoners held here during Salazar's Estado Novo.
- Opened 25 April 2015 (41st anniversary of Carnation Revolution).
- **Four floors**: building history → dictatorship structure → resistance/torture/isolation cells → anti-colonial struggle + Carnation Revolution.
- **Floor 3 covers the anti-colonial wars** (Angola, Mozambique, Guinea-Bissau) and how the refusal to end the empire brought the regime down. Dictatorship + colonialism + revolution in one visit.

### Museu da Sociedade de Geografia de Lisboa — the hidden colonial museum
- **Rua das Portas de Santo Antão 100, Baixa** (~5 min from Chiado)
- Literally the reorganized collection of Lisbon's former "Colonial Museum" (merged 1892). Collections from Angola, Mozambique, Guinea-Bissau, São Tomé, Cape Verde, Timor, Macau.
- Includes objects from the 1934 and 1940 Portuguese Colonial Exhibitions (Salazar-era propaganda).
- **Free. Mon-Fri 10:00-12:45 / 14:00-16:45.** Guided tours at 10:00 and 15:00.
- The closest thing Lisbon has to a "Museum of Colonialism."

### Museu Nacional de Etnologia — decolonial critique
- **Belém** (requires tram 15 or train)
- 40,000+ ethnographic objects from former colonies.
- Temporary exhibition "Deconstructing Colonialism, Decolonising the Imaginary" (runs ~2024-2026).
- Worth it if making a Belém trip anyway.

### Museu do Oriente
- **Alcântara**, former colonial warehouse.
- Portuguese colonial presence in Asia: Goa, Macau, Timor, Malacca. Rare maps, crucifixes, colonial artifacts.

### What doesn't exist
- No Museum of Communism in Lisbon. PCP history is told through the Aljube.
- No formal "Museum of Dictatorship" — Aljube is the closest.
- Dedicated Colonial/Slavery Museum has been debated for years but doesn't exist yet.

## Carnation Revolution — Stand Where It Happened

### Largo do Carmo — the single most important Revolution site
- On 25 April 1974, PM Marcelo Caetano took refuge in the GNR (National Republican Guard) HQ on this square.
- Surrounded by MFA rebel soldiers led by Captain Salgueiro Maia.
- At 17:45, General Spínola arrived to accept Caetano's surrender. **The dictatorship ended here.**
- The GNR building still functions; small GNR Museum inside is visitable.
- Adjacent to the roofless Carmo Convent ruins (1755 earthquake memorial).
- Today it's a pleasant café terrace with jacaranda trees. No museum — just a plaque. The contrast is part of the experience.

### Other Revolution spots
- **Rua do Arsenal** — Salgueiro Maia's tank column entered central Lisbon here
- **Terreiro do Paço** — first MFA unit massed before moving on the government
- **Rossio railway station** — news first broke to civilians here
- All within 10 min walk of Largo do Carmo

## Weird / Niche Museums

### Igreja de São Roque — most expensive chapel in Europe
- **Largo Trindade Coelho, Bairro Alto** — 2 min from Chiado
- Bland white facade outside, insane Baroque interior. Survived 1755 earthquake.
- **Chapel of St. John the Baptist**: built in ROME in 1742, made of lapis lazuli, ivory, amethyst, alabaster, agate, gold, silver. Blessed by the Pope, dismantled, shipped to Lisbon on 3 ships, reassembled. Free to enter.

### Museu Arqueológico do Carmo — Peruvian mummies in a roofless convent
- **Largo do Carmo** — 5 min from Chiado
- The Carmo Convent: most iconic 1755 earthquake ruin (sky is the ceiling).
- Inside: **two 16th-century Peruvian mummies** (boy and girl), shrunken heads, Visigoth belt, 4th-century sarcophagi, Egyptian fragments. Eclectic colonial loot.

### Museu da Farmácia — pharmacy museum
- **Rua Marechal Saldanha 1** (Santa Catarina / Bica area)
- Tuesday 10:00-18:00, €5.
- 5,000+ years of pharmaceutical history: ancient Egyptian pills, 16th-century pill press, NASA astronaut medication kits, a pharmacy that survived Hiroshima, a complete **19th-century Chinese drugstore from Macau**.
- One of Lisbon's best-kept secret museums.

### Galerias Romanas da Rua da Prata — underground Roman city
- Roman cryptoporticus from the reign of Emperor Augustus, buried under Rua da Prata in Baixa.
- **Only open 8 days/year** — 4 in April, 4 in September (usually around April 18 International Monuments Day).
- Tickets via Blueticket, free/€3, sell out in hours.
- One of ~500 people/year who get in. Check Museu de Lisboa website for dates.

### Casa do Alentejo — Moorish-palace freebie
- **Rua das Portas de Santo Antão 58, Restauradores** — same street as Sociedade de Geografia.
- 17th-century palace, now Alentejo regional social club. Walk in the front door for free.
- **Neo-Moorish tiled courtyard**, grand staircases, painted ceilings. Most visitors never realize it's open.
- Cheap Alentejo restaurant upstairs (migas, açorda, carne de porco à alentejana).

## Tuesday-Specific Cluster: Feira da Ladra + São Vicente + Panteão

### Feira da Ladra — Tuesday-only flea market
- **Campo de Santa Clara**, behind São Vicente de Fora monastery.
- **Tuesdays and Saturdays only**, 9:00-18:00 (best before 11am, vendors pack up ~14:00).
- Vinyl, old azulejo tiles, vintage clothes, colonial-era postcards, army surplus, bric-a-brac. Cash only.

### Igreja / Mosteiro de São Vicente de Fora — right next to the market
- Founded 1147, rebuilt 1582 by architect of El Escorial.
- **Cloister has 100,000 18th-century azulejo tiles** — world's largest Baroque tile collection. 38 panels depicting La Fontaine's fables.
- **Braganza Royal Pantheon**: every Portuguese king from João IV (1656) to Manuel II (1932).
- Rooftop terrace with panoramic views. Closed Mondays, open Tuesdays.

### Panteão Nacional (Santa Engrácia) — 5 min walk
- Biggest dome in Lisbon. Construction took 284 years (finished 1966).
- **Tombs**: Amália Rodrigues (fado queen), Eusébio (football legend), Sophia de Mello Breyner, Humberto Delgado (presidential candidate assassinated by PIDE 1965).
- 187-step climb to dome terrace: one of the best 360° views of Lisbon. Closed Mondays, open Tuesdays.

## Street Art

### Key artists
- **Bordalo II** (Lisbon, 1987) — "Big Trash Animals": massive animal sculptures from scrap metal/plastic/waste. Fox on Av. 24 de Julho (Santos), racoon near Centro Cultural de Belém, giant bee at LX Factory.
- **Vhils** (1987) — reverse graffiti: portraits scratched/chipped/drilled INTO walls. Biggest concentration at Fábrica Braço de Prata (Marvila).

### Best neighborhoods
- **Mouraria** — birthplace of authentic Lisbon street art. Mariza mural on Escadinhas de São Cristóvão.
- **Marvila + Beato** — emerging industrial creative zones, huge warehouse murals, galleries, craft breweries.
- **Graça** — large-scale murals with social commentary.
- **Bairro Alto** — constantly evolving tags and paste-ups.

### LX Factory — Alcântara
- Converted 1864 textile factory. Creative hub: studios, printing presses, bookshops, cafés, street art.
- **Livraria Ler Devagar ("Read Slowly")** — one of the most beautiful bookshops in the world. 50,000+ books, flying bicycle sculpture, onsite printing press, café-bar, vinyl shop.
- Bordalo II's giant bee is here.
- Tram 15E from Cais do Sodré or Praça da Figueira to Alcântara-Mar (~15 min).

### Fábrica Braço de Prata — Marvila
- Converted armaments factory (2007). 12 rooms: bookshop, galleries, concerts, bars, garden.
- Highest concentration of Vhils works in the city. Far less touristy than LX Factory.

## African Lisbon

### Largo de São Domingos — the living center, right in Baixa
- Square in front of Igreja de São Domingos (fire-damaged 1959, blackened interior preserved). Main gathering point of the African diaspora in central Lisbon — Cape Verdean, Angolan, Guinean. Kriolu spoken, dominoes, daily gathering. Next to Ginjinha Espinheira.

### Cova da Moura ("Kova M") — for the seriously curious
- Cape Verdean-majority neighborhood in Amadora (15-20 min by train to Damaia). Kriolu is the lingua franca. Unauthorized favela from 1970s, built by migrants during/after independence wars.
- Street art of Mandela, MLK, Tupac, African liberation leaders. KOVARTJAM graffiti festival.
- Music: funana, koladera, Kriolu rap, Afro-house. "Lisbon's hip-hop capital."
- Best visited with a community-run walking tour.

## Gardens, Cemeteries, Hidden Green

### Cemitério dos Prazeres
- Founded 1833 after cholera epidemic. Modeled on Père Lachaise (Paris map/regulations requested in 1838).
- Aristocratic mausoleums, architect-designed tombs. Fernando Pessoa was originally buried here (moved to Jerónimos 1985); fiancée Ofélia's tomb remains.
- Panoramic views over Tagus and 25 de Abril bridge. At tram 28 terminus (Campo de Ourique).

### Jardim do Príncipe Real
- Leafy square with 150-year-old cedar trained flat by metal posts into a massive natural umbrella. Quiosque with drinks underneath.

### Estufa Fria (Parque Eduardo VII)
- "Cold greenhouse" — three greenhouses in a former basalt quarry (1933). Wooden-slat ceilings (no glass), tropical plants, ponds. ~€5, often empty. Near Marquês de Pombal metro.

### Jardim Botânico da Universidade de Lisboa
- Founded 1878. Hidden behind the Natural History Museum off Príncipe Real. Tropical trees from the Portuguese empire.

## Literary Lisbon

### Livraria Bertrand — Rua Garrett 73, Chiado
- **World's oldest bookshop still in operation** — founded 1732, Guinness-certified. Original 1773 rooms preserved. Buy a book, get the "oldest bookshop" stamp.

### Livraria Simão — Escadinhas de São Cristóvão, Mouraria
- "World's smallest bookshop" — 4m², 4,000+ books floor-to-ceiling, fits one customer at a time. Same staircase as the Mariza mural.

### Casa Fernando Pessoa — Rua Coelho da Rocha 16, Campo de Ourique
- Pessoa's last apartment (1920-1935). Preserved as museum/library: original furniture, manuscripts, desk, glasses.

## Quirky Engineering — Funiculars + Elevator

- **Ascensor da Bica** (1892) — most scenic, steep Bica slope. Near Santa Catarina.
- **Ascensor da Glória** (1885) — Restauradores to Miradouro de São Pedro de Alcântara. Touristy.
- **Ascensor do Lavra** (1884) — oldest, least touristy, almost nobody rides it.
- **Elevador de Santa Justa** (1902) — vertical iron elevator by Eiffel student. Neo-Gothic, 45m. Lifehack: walk to Largo do Carmo and access the upper platform from the back (~€1.50 for view only, bypasses the ground-level 45-min queue).
